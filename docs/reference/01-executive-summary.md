# 01 — Executive Summary

## Purpose of the certification

*OVHcloud - Public Cloud - Core Associate* is the entry-level professional certification of the **Public Cloud Core** program. It validates that a learner can independently deploy, secure, and operate a production-grade workload on the **Core layer of OVHcloud Public Cloud** — the IaaS services that are natively built on OpenStack (Compute, Storage, Network, Identity & Security, KMS) along with adjacent services indispensable to a working IaaS stack (Object Storage S3-compatible, vRack, Load Balancer) and their native operation tooling (OpenStack CLI, Terraform with OVHcloud and OpenStack providers).

The certification addresses two co-primary learner personas: **Corporate** organizations (large enterprises and digitally-mature mid-market firms with structured DevOps teams) and **Digital Starters** (SMEs, freelancers, and entrepreneurs operating in self-service mode). Both audiences follow the **same curriculum** with a **shared red-thread scenario**.

## Business value

| Audience | Value |
|---|---|
| **Corporate clients** | Accelerate Public Cloud adoption inside the organization; reduce internal blocking points related to OpenStack unfamiliarity, isolation concerns, and hyperscaler feature-parity anxiety. Provides a structured alternative to the current "internal experts sent reluctantly to training duty" model. |
| **Digital Starters** | Make OVHcloud Public Cloud accessible to small business owners and freelancers operating without dedicated IT support, by structuring entry-level operational competence around a clear progression. |
| **OVHcloud** | Drives Public Cloud Core adoption — the IaaS foundation on which all other Public Cloud certifications (MKS, AI, DBaaS, Analytics, etc.) ultimately depend. Feeds the *Professional Services* bundle with structured trainer-led delivery. Generates demand-side leads through Tech Labs derived from the curriculum. |

## What the program contains

- **8 knowledge domains** mapped to the Core layer of OVHcloud Public Cloud.
- **110 certifiable Learning Outcomes** distributed across Knowledge (45), Skills (47), and Attitudes/postures (18).
- **11 instructor-led modules of 1h30 each**, delivered over **3 days**, with a **1h30 multiple-choice final exam** and a pre-exam wrap-up session.
- **One red-thread case study** (*Northwind Analytics*) running across all modules to anchor concepts in a realistic narrative.
- **Hands-on Tech Lab segments** integrated into each module, with reusable lab artifacts that can be repackaged as standalone Tech Labs for lead generation.

## What the program does not contain

- Specialty tracks (Kubernetes, AI/ML, Analytics, HPC, Quantum, Sovereign advanced topics, DBaaS including PostgreSQL Managed) — addressed by separate certifications.
- Advanced architecture (high availability design, multi-region disaster recovery, FinOps optimization) — addressed by *OVHcloud - Public Cloud - Core Professional* (future).
- Application development on top of cloud services — out of scope by design (the learner is an operator, not a developer).

## Delivery format and commercial path

- **Format**: Instructor-Led, in-person, 3 consecutive days.
- **Channels**: distributed primarily through the OVHcloud *Professional Services* bundle; also bundled with corporate Enterprise support contracts where applicable; differentiated access for Corporate clients (paid, often with dedicated AM) and Digital Starters (subsidized track in line with the no-dedicated-AM segment).
- **Learner cost**: training credits provided as vouchers; no personal payment method required from the learner.
- **Examination**: 1h30 multiple-choice, 70% passing score (industry-aligned with AWS Cloud Practitioner and Azure Fundamentals).
